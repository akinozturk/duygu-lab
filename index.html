Welcome to the Lab

Decoding Molecular Control of Gene Expression

The Ozturk Research Group investigates the fundamental mechanisms of **post-transcriptional regulation**, focusing on how key protein complexes control mRNA translation, stability, and decay in development and disease.

Explore Our Work

Dr. Duygu Kuzuoglu Ozturk

Principal Investigator & Molecular Biologist

Dr. Ozturk leads the group with expertise in molecular and cell biology, focusing on the mechanisms that control **mRNA fate and protein synthesis**. Her work identifies crucial regulatory steps and protein complexes that, when dysregulated, contribute to diseases like cancer and neurological disorders. Our Core Research Areas

We investigate the molecular components and regulatory networks that govern mRNA expression and stability.

RNA Decay & CCR4-NOT

Mechanistically dissecting the **CCR4-NOT complex**, a key regulator of mRNA deadenylation and decay. We study its structure, assembly, and role in miRNA-mediated target silencing and general mRNA turnover.

Translational Control in Cancer

Investigating how oncogenes like **N-myc** hijack the translational machinery (e.g., eIF4F complex, mTOR) to promote cancer cell fitness and identifying small-molecule inhibitors to disrupt these pathways.

üî¨

Molecular Disease Mechanisms

Exploring the role of metabolic enzymes (like PHGDH) and protein-protein interactions in disease etiology, including viral infections (e.g., SARS-CoV-2) and tissue regeneration models.
